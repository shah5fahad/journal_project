Scope Of Journal

Curevita Innovation of BioData Intelligence (CIBDI) - An National Peer-Reviewed Multidisciplinary Journal

Health & Biomedical Sciences

Biomedical & Healthcare Data Science

  • Application of AI and machine learning for disease prediction, diagnosis, and personalized medicine
  • Big data integration from India's large-scale health initiatives (e.g., Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission, National Cancer Registry)
  • Genomic medicine research tailored for India's genetically diverse population

Genomics & Biotechnology

Biological & Genomic Data Intelligence

  • Development of computational models for genome sequencing projects like the IndiGen Genome Project
  • Complex network analysis of genomes, proteomes, and metabolomes relevant to Indian biodiversity
  • Integration of bioinformatics in agricultural biotechnology, plant genomics, and livestock improvement

Public Health

AI & Data Analytics in Public Health

  • Epidemiological modeling for infectious diseases (e.g., tuberculosis, malaria, COVID-19)
  • AI-enabled prediction systems for outbreaks in high-risk regions
  • Health informatics and data-driven strategies to strengthen India's health system

Agriculture & Food Systems

Agri-Bioinformatics & Food Systems

  • Application of big data analytics to improve crop productivity, soil health, and climate-resilient farming
  • AI-driven livestock monitoring and disease management in India's dairy and poultry sectors
  • Genomic and phenomic databases for indigenous crop varieties and breeds

Environmental Science

Environmental & Ecological Data Science

  • AI and data modeling for climate change impacts, biodiversity conservation, and ecosystem resilience in India
  • Satellite-based big data analytics for monitoring agriculture, forests, and water resources
  • Complex network modeling for river ecology, air quality, and environmental health

Digital Infrastructure

Digital Infrastructure & Policy Dimensions

  • Leveraging India's expanding digital health and bioinformatics infrastructure (e.g., iNDIAB, IndiaStack, CoWIN data ecosystem)
  • Data ethics, governance, privacy, and security frameworks tailored for Indian biomedicine and agriculture
  • Enabling policies for open science, FAIR data principles (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable), and equitable access

National Relevance & Objectives

Harnessing India's Data Advantage

  • Harnessing India’s Data Advantage: With its vast population, genomic diversity, agricultural heterogeneity, and rapidly growing digital health ecosystem, India is a unique hub for biological big data research.
  • Driving Innovation: Promotes collaboration across India's premier research institutes (IITs, IISc, AIIMS, ICAR, CSIR labs, DBT institutes) and startups working at the interface of AI, biology, and data science.
  • Strengthening Policy & Practice: Provides evidence to guide national strategies in precision medicine, agricultural innovation, environmental monitoring, and public health resilience.
  • Inclusive Capacity Building: Encourages participation from regional universities, young researchers, and interdisciplinary innovators across India to democratize biodata research.

Bioinformatics & Computational Biology

Biological Data Science & Analytics

  • Bioinformatics & Computational Biology: Algorithm development, statistical modeling, and software tools for analyzing genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, and metabolomics data
  • Biological Big Data Management: Efficient storage, integration, and retrieval of large-scale biological datasets from high-throughput experiments and imaging platforms
  • Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning in Biology: Predictive modeling, pattern recognition, and deep learning applications for disease prediction, drug discovery, and biomarker identification
  • Biomedical Image Analysis: AI-driven interpretation of medical, histological, and microscopic imaging for diagnostics and research
  • Systems Biology & Network Analytics: Modeling and simulation of biological systems, molecular interactions, and pathway networks
  • Precision & Predictive Healthcare Analytics: Data-driven approaches for personalized medicine, clinical decision-making, and epidemiological forecasting.
  • Agricultural & Environmental Bioanalytics: Big data applications in biodiversity assessment, crop genomics, climate change modeling, and sustainable agriculture.
  • Ethics, Standards, and FAIR Data Principles: Best practices in biological data sharing, governance, reproducibility, and privacy protection.
  • Interdisciplinary Data Science, AI, and Network Solutions: The practical applications in data science, machine learning, big data analytics, networks, and AI-driven systems. It covers topics from data engineering and visualization to network security, IoT, and communication technologies, fostering interdisciplinary work that links data science with intelligent network solutions for real-world impact.
  • Computer science, Data Science and Artificial Intelligence: The practical applications in data science, machine learning, big data analytics, networks, and AI-driven systems. It covers topics from data engineering and visualization to network security, IoT, and communication technologies, fostering interdisciplinary work that links data science with intelligent network solutions for real-world impact.

Network Science & Analytics

Complex Network Analysis

  • Network Science & Graph Analytics: Exploration of graph theory, network topology, and algorithms for analyzing large-scale interconnected data
  • Social & Information Networks: Data-driven modeling of online communities, knowledge sharing, and information diffusion across digital platforms
  • Biological & Biomedical Networks: Systems biology, protein–protein interactions, gene regulatory networks, and disease association networks
  • AI & Machine Learning for Networks: Application of deep learning, graph neural networks (GNNs), and reinforcement learning in complex networks
  • Communication & Sensor Networks: Big data analytics in IoT, wireless sensor networks, and next-generation communication systems
  • Cybersecurity & Trust Networks: Network modeling for anomaly detection, intrusion prediction, and secure data sharing.
  • Epidemiological & Health Networks: Modeling disease spread, patient referral systems, and healthcare service optimization.
  • Smart Cities & Infrastructure Networks: Integration of data science for transportation, energy, and urban system connectivity.
  • Ethics & Responsible Networking: Ensuring transparency, privacy, and fairness in network data analysis and applications.
  • Mathematical Biology: Population Dynamics, Epidemiological Models, Cellular and Molecular Biology Models, Ecological Models, Neuroscience Models, Mathematical Models in Biological Systems* (* not limited to that only).

Genomic Applications

Genomic Network Analysis

  • Gene Co-Expression Networks: Construction of networks where nodes represent genes, and edges represent co-expression relationships, enabling the discovery of functional gene modules
  • Regulatory Networks: Modeling transcription factor–gene interactions to understand the regulation of gene expression and identify master regulators
  • Protein–Protein Interaction (PPI) Networks: Linking genome-derived proteins into complex networks to reveal functional pathways, disease associations, and potential drug targets
  • Metabolic & Pathway Networks: Mapping genomic information to metabolic pathways to study system-level organization of cellular processes
  • Graph-Based Genome Assembly: Use of de Bruijn graphs and overlap-layout-consensus methods for reconstructing genomes from high-throughput sequencing data
  • Disease Gene Networks: Network medicine approaches that connect genetic variants to disease phenotypes through shared biological pathways.
  • Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) in Genomics: Application of deep learning over complex biological networks for genome annotation, variant prediction, and functional inference.

Submission Guidelines

Ensure your manuscript follows the required format before submitting to Curevita Innovation of BioData Intelligence (CIBDI)

Types of Submissions

What You Can Submit

  • Original Research Articles
  • Review Articles
  • Case Studies
  • Short Communications
  • Conceptual Papers
  • Editorials and Opinion Pieces

Manuscript Preparation

Formatting & Structure

  • Language: English (British or American)
  • Font: Times New Roman, 12pt, 1.5 spacing
  • Length: 3,000–7,000 words
  • Structure:
    • Title, Abstract (150–300 words), Keywords (4–6)
    • Introduction, Methodology, Results & Discussion
    • Conclusion, References (APA/MLA/IEEE)
  • Figures & Tables: High resolution, clearly labeled

Submission Process

How to Submit

  • Submit via Email or Submission Portal
  • Email: infocvrb@curevita.org
  • Include Cover Letter with:
    • Title of the manuscript
    • Author names and affiliations
    • Corresponding author's contact details
    • Declaration of originality
    • Conflict of interest statement

Publication Policies

Our commitment to quality, transparency, and ethical publishing practices.

Peer Review Policy

We follow a double-blind peer review process. Each submission is reviewed by at least two experts. Final decisions are made by the editorial team based on the reviewers’ evaluations. Typical review time is 2–4 weeks.

Ethical Guidelines

We adhere to COPE standards. Authors must ensure originality, cite all sources, disclose conflicts of interest, and get ethical approval for human/animal studies.

Publication Fee

A nominal Article Processing Charge (APC)- 10,000 INR and for international 148 USD may apply to support open-access dissemination.

Copyright & Licensing

Authors retain copyright of their work. Publications are licensed under Creative Commons (CC BY), unless stated otherwise.

Indexing & Archiving

Articles receive a DOI and are archived permanently. Indexing in reputed databases is ensured through active partnerships.

Plagiarism Policy

Manuscripts must be original. We screen for plagiarism and reject those with over 15% similarity (excluding references). Self-plagiarism is not allowed.

Membership Policy

Annual Author Membership for Indian Author is ₹12,000 INR and for International author is $148 USD.

Membership Privileges
  • Publication Support: Up to four (4) articles published free of cost annually.
  • Academic Engagement: Exclusive access to Curevita’s research webinars, workshops, faculty development programs (FDPs), seminars, and symposia.
  • Leadership Opportunities: Participation in editorial boards and scholarly committees within Curevita Journals.

Withdrawal & Retraction

Authors can withdraw before acceptance via email. After acceptance, justification is required. Retractions are issued for ethical violations post-publication.

Complaints & Appeals

Complaints regarding editorial decisions, peer review, or ethical issues can be formally submitted. Each case will be independently reviewed by our team.
